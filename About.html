ABOUT US

We are a team of 5 sophomores from Olin College of Engineering! 
L.A.D was our final project in Principles of Integrated Engineering, a course all about integrating the mechanical, electrical, and software components of engineering. 

Read more about each of us below!

Elisa

Elisa is a Mechanical Engineering student who worked as the project manager and a part of the Mechanical subteam. She was in charge of the body and outer shell, as well as designing mounts for electrical components and focusing on integrating mechanical and electrical systems within the torso. She once lost L.A.D's arm because the acrylic she chose was too clear.

Mia

Mia is an Electrical and Computer Engineering student with interests in power management and circuit design. She designed and implemented the robot's electrical system, overcoming challenges such as managing separate power supplies at different currents, simultaneously controlling 8 servos, and wire harnessing around their joints. She has significantly less hair than at the beginning of the project due to soldering incidents.

Zara

Zara is an Engineering with Computing student interested in robotics and high level software design. She developed the pose tracking functionality and figured out how to calculate the angles between each part of the user's arm. She now has recurring dreams about arctangents.

Mika

Mika is a Mechanical Engineering (?) student who decided to explore her interest in software during this project. She worked on controlling the motors and LEDs, as well as supporting Zara with the pose tracking. She strongly considered adding Terminator mode to L.A.D after a certain CA stepped on the shell.
