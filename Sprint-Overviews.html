Sprint Overview

This project was split up into 3 2-week sprints and 1 1-week sprint. Here's what we accomplished at the end of each sprint.

Sprint 1

What Did We Do?

In this sprint, we managed to assemble one arm of L.A.D! We started by speccing servo motors and buying a Raspberry Pi and Pi Camera, then set up the Pi with the standard Pi OS. We designed and 3D printed 2 pan-tilt mechanisms, one for the shoulder and one for the elbow, and laser cut the forearm and bicep out of acrylic. We figured out that we needed to use wall power for the Raspberry Pi, and we managed to get the motors to move using a servo hat and code run on the Pi. We also made a movement detection program that used the Pi camera that would print to the terminal if it saw movement. However, the arm itself was not yet controlled by whatever the camera detected.

Sprint 2

What Did We Do?

In this sprint, we laser cut the first iteration of L.A.D's full torso and arms, and built both of the arms with the same pan-tilt mechanism as Sprint 1. We added 4 more servos, bringing the total count up to 8, and figured out how to house the wiring and power supply within the torso. We also wrote a script that used the Pi camera, OpenCV, and Mediapipe to track someone's pose and calculate the angles of their arm compared to their torso. We also got initial integration working, with servos moving as the user moved, although not the correct servos.

Sprint 3

What Did We Do?

In this sprint, we completed the CAD models for the body of L.A.D - we adjusted the shoulders to be more proportional to an actual human being and created the shell of L.A.D (head, torso, arms, and hands). We also changed our servo positioning from 2 servos at both the shoulders and elbows to 3 servos at the shoulder and 1 at the elbow, in order to more accurately mimic human motion. We fixed the servos so that they would all rotate in the correct direction, and recut and wired up the body, to create a working prototype! We started to use a battery pack to power the Pi while still using wall power for the servo hat, and began to test 3D movement, both in the software and on the robot itself.

Sprint 3.5

What Did We Do?

In this sprint, we printed out the head, hands, and torso shell and integrated them onto L.A.D. We implemented LEDs to show the user when they were in frame of the camera, and ensured that L.A.D would only copy people's movements when they were in frame. We finished wire harnessing everything and screwed in all of the servo horns and bolts with locknuts to ensure everything would stay in place. This sprint culminated in Demo Day, when we got to let the community try out our robot!
